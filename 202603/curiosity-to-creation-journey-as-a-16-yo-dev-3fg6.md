<p>When I first saw programming, I didn’t really understand it. I just saw people writing strange words on a screen and somehow computers listened to them.</p>

<p>The first thing I ever learned was this:</p>

<p>console.log("Hello World")</p>

<p>At first it didn’t look like anything special. But when it printed words on the screen, I thought it was really cool.</p>

<p>*<em>The Beginning<br>
*</em><br>
I have always liked technology and things about the future like AI and robots. I didn’t have the best devices or tools, but that didn’t stop me from trying to learn.</p>

<p>So I started watching tutorials and reading things online. Sometimes I understood them, sometimes I didn’t.</p>

<p>*<em>Learning Was Not Easy<br>
*</em><br>
When I started learning JavaScript, many things were confusing.</p>

<p>Things like:</p>

<p>functions</p>

<p>arrays</p>

<p>promises</p>

<p>Sometimes my code didn’t work and I didn’t know why. I would stare at the screen for a long time trying to find the mistake.</p>

<p>But slowly it started making sense.</p>

<p>Small Projects</p>

<p>To practice, I started building small things.</p>

<p>I made things like:</p>

<p>a compliment generator</p>

<p>a mood checker</p>

<p>a to-do list</p>

<p>an Avengers themed login idea</p>

<p>They were small projects but they made me feel proud because I built them myself.</p>

<p>Why I Like Coding</p>

<p>The thing I like about coding is that it lets you create things from ideas in your head.</p>

<p>You imagine something, then you try to build it with code. Sometimes it works, sometimes it breaks, but you keep trying.</p>

<p>It feels a bit like solving puzzles.</p>

<p>What I Am Doing Now</p>

<p>Now I am working on something bigger.</p>

<p>I am building my first SaaS called Book Chap. The idea is to use AI to explain complex topics in a way that is easier to understand. Sometimes textbooks or lessons can feel confusing, so I want to make something that helps students learn faster.</p>

<p>I started this project when I was 16 and it is one of the biggest things I have tried to build so far.</p>

<p>What I Learned</p>

<p>One big thing I learned is that you don’t have to know everything to start.</p>

<p>You just need to be curious and keep trying.</p>

<p>Every small thing you learn adds up.</p>

<p>For Anyone Starting</p>

<p>If you are starting to learn coding, it might feel hard at first.</p>

<p>That’s normal.</p>

<p>Just keep building small things and don’t give up when things break. That is actually part of learning.</p>

<p>One day you will look back and realize how much you have improved.</p>
