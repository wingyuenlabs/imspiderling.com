<p>AI is changing the way we work as developers.</p>

<p>We are moving from writing code to <strong>writing specifications</strong>. Instead of implementing features ourselves, we rely more on <strong>agent modes</strong> and tools like Claude Code, Codex, or Gemini CLI.</p>

<p>This works incredibly well. Productivity increases, repetitive work disappears, and we can ship features faster.</p>

<p>But there is something I’ve started noticing.</p>

<p>Developers are not necessarily learning more.<br>
We are just <strong>doing the same things faster</strong>.</p>

<h2>
  
  
   AI Cannot Replace Understanding
</h2>

<p>When you rely heavily on AI tools, something subtle happens.</p>

<p>You stop exploring new solutions yourself.<br>
You stop experimenting.<br>
You stop learning new paradigms.</p>

<p>Many developers simply apply the same solutions they always used, just faster because AI writes the code.</p>

<p>When a new approach or development practice appears, it often does not get adopted well because the developer does not really understand it.</p>

<p>AI can generate code.</p>

<p>But it cannot give you <strong>knowledge you never built</strong>.</p>

<h2>
  
  
  The Knowledge Gap
</h2>

<p>A simple example.</p>

<p>I am not an expert in:</p>

<ul>
<li>load testing</li>
<li>monitoring</li>
<li>observability</li>
</ul>

<p>I know these areas are important. But if I do not understand the concepts, how am I supposed to apply them correctly?</p>

<p>Even with the best prompts and agents, the result will still be shallow.</p>

<p>AI helps execute ideas. It does not replace learning them.</p>

<h2>
  
  
  Where This Shows the Most
</h2>

<p>I notice this especially in two areas.</p>

<ul>
<li>testing</li>
<li>accessibility</li>
</ul>

<h3>
  
  
  Testing
</h3>

<p>Recently I have heard things like:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>Why write tests if AI generates almost perfect code?</p>
</blockquote>

<p>or:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>We run Playwright tests with AI after the feature. That is enough.</p>
</blockquote>

<p>But testing is not about validating one run. It exists to prevent regressions, catch old bugs, and protect systems as they evolve.</p>

<p>AI can generate tests.</p>

<p>But <strong>testing strategy still requires understanding.</strong></p>

<h3>
  
  
  Accessibility
</h3>

<p>Accessibility has always been ignored in many projects.</p>

<p>AI has not changed that.</p>

<p>You can add an accessibility agent, but accessibility is not just adding attributes. It requires understanding how assistive technologies behave, how screen readers navigate, and how keyboard interaction works.</p>

<p>Without that knowledge, accessibility stays superficial.</p>

<h2>
  
  
  The New Developer Loop
</h2>

<p>Something interesting is happening.</p>

<p>Developers spend more time writing Markdown specs for AI than learning new technical concepts.</p>

<p>Instead of experimenting, breaking things, and learning through failure, we optimise prompts and agent workflows.</p>

<p>But not necessarily <strong>ourselves</strong>.</p>

<h2>
  
  
  I Am Also In That Loop
</h2>

<p>To be fair, I am also part of this.</p>

<p>AI tools are powerful and it is easy to rely on them.</p>

<p>But recently I started asking myself a simple question.</p>

<p>Am I actually improving?</p>

<p>Or am I just getting faster?</p>

<p>So I am trying to go back to learning new concepts, experimenting more, and understanding systems deeper.</p>

<p>And only after that, delegating to AI.</p>

<h2>
  
  
  Ask Yourself This
</h2>

<p>If AI makes you <strong>10x more productive</strong>, ask yourself:</p>

<p>Are you doing something you would never do if you had infinite time?</p>

<p>Are you exploring areas you previously did not understand?</p>

<p>Or are you just shipping the same things faster?</p>

<p>And one more question.</p>

<p>If your productivity increases 10x, do you now have 10x more time?</p>

<p>More time to learn, exercise, spend time with family, or build deeper knowledge?</p>

<p>If the answer is no, then what is AI really doing for you?</p>

<p>Is it helping you become a better developer?</p>

<p>Or just a faster version of the same developer?</p>
