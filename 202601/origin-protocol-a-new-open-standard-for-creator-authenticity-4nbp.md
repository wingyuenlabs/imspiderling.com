<p>Protecting creators, restoring trust, and building the foundation for a safer digital world.</p>

<p>Creators are losing ownership of their work. Platforms are overwhelmed by impersonation, stolen content, and synthetic media. And the tools we rely on — UUIDs, Ed25519 keys, OAuth identity, even blockchain signatures — were never designed for the world we’re living in now.<br>
So I built something new.<br>
Origin Protocol is an open, platform‑agnostic standard for proving who created what, when, and how — without requiring creators to understand cryptography or trust any single company.<br>
It’s simple, tamper‑evident, and ready for platforms to adopt today.<br>
— -<br>
Why I built Origin<br>
I’m a solo developer with limited resources, but I care deeply about creators — especially the ones who don’t have teams, lawyers, or money to protect their work. I wanted to build something that:<br>
gives creators a verifiable signature on every piece of content<br>
• gives platforms a trustworthy verification path<br>
• gives the public a way to know what’s real<br>
• works across any platform, any format, any ecosystem<br>
And it had to be open.<br>
No lock‑in.<br>
No gatekeeping.<br>
No hidden dependencies.<br>
Just a standard anyone can adopt.<br>
— -<br>
What Origin does<br>
Origin provides:<br>
a creator app that seals content with a cryptographic manifest<br>
• a platform SDK for verifying authenticity<br>
• a ledger API for checking revocation, key status, and policy<br>
• a governance bootstrap for trust anchoring<br>
• a test suite for platform integration<br>
• a policy system for strict, standard, or permissive verification<br>
Everything is documented, versioned, and ready for real‑world use.<br>
— -<br>
What makes Origin different<br>
Origin isn’t a blockchain.<br>
It isn’t a token.<br>
It isn’t a walled garden.<br>
Become a member<br>
It’s a protocol, not a product.<br>
No fees<br>
• No tokens<br>
• No dependencies<br>
• No platform lock‑in<br>
• No proprietary algorithms<br>
Just a clean, open, verifiable standard.<br>
— -<br>
The experimental layer: LYRA<br>
Alongside Origin, I’ve been developing LYRA, a quantum‑inspired identity model based on:<br>
coherence<br>
• entropy<br>
• harmonic signatures<br>
• state evolution<br>
• spatial coherence grids<br>
It’s experimental, but it represents a possible future beyond static keys and traditional PKI.<br>
Origin uses Ed25519 today — because platforms need stability — but LYRA is where identity could go next.<br>
