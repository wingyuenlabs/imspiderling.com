<h3>
  
  
  1. Brazil - The Red Devils
</h3>

<p>Brazil tops the list as a favorite, and it's hard to argue against their credentials. With an arsenal of skilled players like Neymar, Alisson, and Richarlison, they possess a formidable attacking force. However, their defensive vulnerabilities have been exposed in recent tournaments.</p>

<h3>
  
  
  2. Argentina - The Albiceleste
</h3>

<p>Argentina's Lionel Messi is a once-in-a-generation talent, but the team's overall depth remains a concern. A strong midfield presence, coupled with Messi's creativity, could propel them to victory.</p>

<h3>
  
  
  3. France - Les Bleus
</h3>

<p>France has consistently punched above their weight in major tournaments. Their attacking prowess, spearheaded by Kylian Mbappé and Antoine Griezmann, is matched only by Brazil's. However, a relatively weak defense may hinder their progress.</p>
